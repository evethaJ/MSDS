2023-08-18

Introduction

In this report I correlate the severity of NYPD Shooting Incident(Fatal, Non-fatal) with administrative unit(Borough) and victims demographics(Age, Sex and Race).

Importing and summarize the data

The variables that I used in the analysis appeared not to have any missing values. Here is the list of variables and their corresponding description that I used in the analysis. - BORO: Borough where the incident occurred. - OCCUR_DATE: Month, day, and year in which the shooting incident occurred. - OCCUR_TIME: Time of day in which the shooting incident occurred. - STATISTCAL_MUREDER_FLAG: Record stating whether a shooting resulted in the victims death. - VIC_SEX: Victims gender - VIC_RACE: Victims race as categorized by the New York City Police Department. - VIC_AGE_GROUP: Victims age group as categorized by the New York City Police Department.

Mutate some variables for analysis

  • I created variables such as hour, year and incident type that I used later in the analysis.
data_a <- data %>%
  mutate(hour = as.numeric(substr(OCCUR_TIME, 1,2)),
         year = year(mdy(OCCUR_DATE)),
         categorized_hour = cut(hour,
                                 breaks = c(0, 6, 12, 18, 23),
                                 labels = c('0-6', '7-12', '13-18', '19-23'),
                                 include.lowest = TRUE),
         incident_type = ifelse(STATISTICAL_MURDER_FLAG, "Fatal Incident", "Non-Fatal Incident"),
         incident_type_model = ifelse(STATISTICAL_MURDER_FLAG, 1, 0)
  )

Fatal and non-fatal incident

  • I created a plot showing the number of fatal and non-fatal incident in NYPD shooting incident data. Fatal incident constituted 19% of the incidents.
data_a %>%
  group_by(incident_type) %>%
  summarise(count = n()) %>%
  mutate(percent = round((count / sum(count)) * 100),3) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = incident_type, y = count, fill = incident_type)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity") +
  geom_text(aes(label = paste(count, "(", percent, "%)")),
            vjust = -0.5, color = "black", size = 3) +
  labs(title = "Incident Types", y='No. of Incidents',
       x='Incident Types', fill='') +
  theme_igray()+
  theme(plot.title = element_text(hjust = 0.5, face = 'bold'),
        legend.position = 'top')

MODELING

  • I used logistic regression to model the relationship between the severity of incident(fatal or non-fatal) and victims demographics(age, gender, race) and administrative units(borough)
  • I used logistic regression because the response variable(fatal vs non-fatal) is binary
  • The results shows age group, and administrative unit may have a significant association with the severity of incident. # Convert categorical variables to dummy variables
data_dummies <- model.matrix(~ hour + VIC_SEX + VIC_RACE + VIC_AGE_GROUP + BORO - 1, data_a)
response_var =data_a$incident_type_model

Fit logistic regression model and summarize

logistic_model <- glm(response_var ~ data_dummies, family = binomial(link = "logit"))
summary(logistic_model)

Source of bias

The NYPD Shooting Incident data, be subject to various sources of bias that can impact the accuracy and representativeness of the reported information. Such bias may include: - Unreported or undocumented incident might lead to an underrepresentation of the true number of shooting incidents. - The dataset may have not included incidents from all neighborhoods or communities equally. - The data had incomplete information in some of the variable which could lead to a lack of context and potential misinterpretation

Conclusion:

In this report, I examined the connection between the severity of NYPD Shooting Incidents (Fatal and Non-fatal) and factors like the administrative unit (Borough) and victim demographics (Age, Sex, and Race). It was found that Fatal incidents made up 19% of all incidents. Here are the key observations based on victim demographics: - The majority of victims were male. - The age group between 18 and 44 had the highest number of incidents. - Incidents involving Black individuals appeared to be overrepresented in both fatal and non-fatal cases. Through trend analysis, the following insights were derived: - Both Fatal and non-fatal incidents were more common during nighttime. - The number of incidents increased from 2009 to 2020, with a noticeable upward trend since 2014.

These findings provide valuable insights into the relationship between incident severity, victim demographics, and temporal trends within the context of NYPD Shooting Incidents.
